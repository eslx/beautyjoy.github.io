Our approach  Online and scalable

Our pedagogical approach has been heavily influenced by contemporary advances regarding the development of listening comprehension in second languages1 and learning through observation.2

Second Language Acquisition research has shown that listening comprehension is perhaps the most important skill for language learners to develop, in great part because it is necessary for taking advantage of future opportunities to learn. Our approach to teaching listening comprehension emphasizes making use of learners’ prior knowledge of familiar situations, and developing strategies that have been shown to facilitate comprehension. We use video materials in Spanish to trigger our learners’ prior knowledge, and then lead them through multiple iterations of predicting and interpreting common situations in English.

Research from the Learning Sciences on observational learning has found that active observation of peers interacting with instructors can lead to substantial learning gains. Our approach involves carefully edited video clips of face-to-face sessions conducted at Escuela Popular by expert instructors for students with profiles similar to our target online learners. Through a combination of multiple camera angles from face-to-face sessions along with pedagogical videos used during those sessions, our lessons provide online learners with guided instruction as they actively engage in the same learning tasks as their observed peers. At the same time, through footage of their observed peers, our online learners are able to reflect on their own predictions and interpretations in relation to those of their peers.

The curricular sample we have included here is a module on parent-teacher conferences that embodies the multiple goals of our listening comprehension curriculum: to teach listening strategies, to provide ample opportunities to practice those strategies, and to provide learners with the parent education they need to support their children’s schooling experiences.
